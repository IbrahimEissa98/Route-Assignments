Pyramids

The Pyramids of Cairo, also known as the Pyramids of Giza, are ancient structures built during the ancient kingdom period in ancient Egypt, around 2580-2560 BC. It was built as tombs of the pharaohs, and the largest pyramid in Giza was the largest with a height of about 146 meters.

Al-Azhar Al-Sharif Mosque

For Islamic architecture enthusiasts, Al-Azhar Mosque is a destination that must be visited. Its foundation dates back to Al-Fatimi Jawhar Al-Sakili in 970, and it now constitutes an amazing mixture of different methods and periods.

Salah Al-Din Al-Ayoubi Castle

Sultan Salah Al-Din Al-Ayoubi Castle, a huge castle in Islamic Cairo, is characterized by a strong presence on top of the hills of Al-Muqtim. It was initially established by Salah al-Din al-Ayoubi in 1176 AD, and it became the official seat of the rulers of Egypt.

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization is the first museum of its kind in Egypt and the Arab world, and it is considered an integrated global civilizational complex.

Opera House

Egypt represents a geographical and cultural meeting point, as it has attracted various civilizations over the ages. In 1869, Al-Khadawi Ismail decided to establish an opera house in honor of the opening of the Suez Canal.

Cairo Tower

Cairo Tower is a television tower located in Cairo, the capital of Egypt. The tower was built in 1961 and officially opened in 1961 as well. The tower is about 187 meters high, and it is considered one of the most prominent tourist attractions in Cairo.
